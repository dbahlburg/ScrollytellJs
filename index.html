Development of Krill

Krill at birth
Antarctic krill start their life as an egg. During the spawning season, each female releases thousands of small eggs. After approximately 30 days, a small nauplius-larva hatches and begins its life in the water column.

Nauplius stage
Nauplius larvae are typical for all marine crustaceans and one of their commonon features. Even barnacles or lobster go through this larval stage in their life cycle. In case of krill, there are five distinct nauplius-stages which the individual goes through within 25 days until it reaches the metanauplius stage.
